Nintendo Entertainment System

An 8-bit third-generation home video game console produced by Nintendo

Super Nintendo Entertainment System

A 16-bit home video game console developed by Nintendo that was released in 1990 in Japan and South Korea and in 1991 for the US


Nintendo 64

A home video game console developed by Nintendo. The successor to the Super Nintendo Entertainment System, it was released on June 23, 1996 in Japan; September 26, 1996 in North America; and on March 1, 1997 in Europe and Australia.


Nintendo Gamecube

A home video game console developed and released by Nintendo in Japan on September 14, 2001, in North America on November 18, 2001, and in PAL territories in 2002. It is the successor to the Nintendo 64 (1996), and predecessor of the Wii (2006).


Nintendo Wii

A home video game console developed and marketed by Nintendo. It was released on November 19, 2006, in North America and in December 2006 for most other regions of the world.


Nintendo Wii U

A home video game console developed by Nintendo as the successor to the Wii. Released in late 2012, it is the first eighth-generation video game console



Nintendo Switch

A hybrid video game console developed by Nintendo and released worldwide in most regions on March 3, 2017. The console itself is a tablet that can either be docked for home console use or used as a portable device
